What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used metric for estimating body fat based on an individual's weight in relation to their height. It helps determine whether a person’s weight falls within a healthy range and is often used as an initial screening tool to identify potential health risks associated with underweight, overweight, or obesity.

However, it’s important to understand that BMI does not directly measure body fat. Several other factors—such as muscle mass, fat distribution, age, sex, and genetic background—can influence BMI results. For example, someone with a muscular build may have a high BMI but low body fat. Therefore, BMI should be considered as one part of a more comprehensive health assessment that includes additional measurements and evaluations.

BMI Categories

BMI values are classified into distinct categories to help evaluate weight status and guide appropriate health actions. These classifications are:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher

How to Calculate BMI

Calculating your BMI is straightforward and can be done using either the metric (SI) system or the imperial system. Here’s how:

  1. Multiply your height by itself (height × height).
  2. Divide your weight by the result from step one.
International System of Units (SI)

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ (height (m))²

Imperial System

BMI = weight (lb) ÷ (height (in))² × 703
